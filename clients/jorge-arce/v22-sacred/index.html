the work

Some surgeons chase volume. Jorge Arce spent twenty-seven years doing the opposite — taking the complex cases other surgeons refer out, and giving each one the time it actually needs.

He grew up in Colombia, in a family that valued precision and patience long before he ever held a scalpel. Medicine was not a career choice so much as a calling — a way to use his hands to give something back.

He moved to California to specialize and never left, devoting his career entirely to oral and maxillofacial surgery and dental implantology. Board certified in implantology.

In 2007 he founded the Dental Implants Center of South Bay, guided by one conviction: difficult work, done with care, gives people their lives back.

dr. jorge arce, d.d.s. — south bay, california

Question is, how do you actually keep surgical work careful and sound?

By refusing shortcuts. By planning every case before anyone operates. By treating each patient as the person they are before the procedure they need.
