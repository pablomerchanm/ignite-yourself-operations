Tracing a Surgeon— the work of Dr. Arce

Every restored smile has a provenance. Trace it back far enough and you arrive at a household in Colombia, where careful work was treated as a form of respect — long before Jorge Arce ever held a scalpel.

He trained as a dentist in Colombia and found the discipline that would hold his attention for a lifetime: surgery of the mouth, face, and jaw. Medicine was not a career choice so much as a calling — a way to use his hands to give something back.

He moved to California to specialize, and never left. The cases that followed him were the ones other clinicians preferred to send elsewhere.

Complex extractions. Reconstruction where bone and tissue have been lost. Full-arch implant restorations for patients who had been told, sometimes more than once, that nothing could be done. Board certified in implantology, he made the difficult cases his ordinary day.

Provenance — three dates that hold the story
1999

The craft begins. A young surgeon devotes himself entirely to oral and maxillofacial surgery — and to nothing else, for the next twenty-seven years.

One discipline, refined across two countries. The specialization would become the throughline of everything that followed.
2007

The Dental Implants Center of South Bay opens its doors, guided by one conviction: difficult work, done with care, gives people their lives back.

Redondo Beach and Torrance, California. Not a volume clinic — a referral destination for the cases that need a surgeon willing to slow down.
Today

A father, a mentor to younger surgeons, and a quiet student of his own craft — still reading, still refining, still treating every patient as a person first.

The record: twenty-seven years, board certification in implantology, six disciplines, two locations.
